spec What runs today vs. the roadmap

Running today

Integration-test-verified against real data.
  • Auto-FTE anomaly agent — 5 detectors: wrong-unit/over-receipt, receiving price-spike vs weighted-average cost, theoretical-vs-actual usage variance, sales anomaly, and loss-making margins.
  • Reactive + proactive — screens every stock-in receipt before approval and sweeps usage/sales/margins; findings carry severity + estimated exposure, deduped and persisted.
  • Out-of-band alerts — branded HTML email + WhatsApp, surfaced on the dashboard feed. "Detected automatically… no one had to open a report."
  • Recipe Advisor with agentic cross-consultation — a chef-bot and a finance-bot consult each other, grounded in real BoM, costing workbook, SOPs and 12 months of sales.
  • Full back-office underneath — menu, inventory with an append-only stock ledger, double-entry accounting, loyalty, HR, reports, RBAC and audit on every mutation.

On the roadmap

From advisory to a true 24/7 employee.
  • Wire a real scheduler — the single change that makes the proactive scan literally 24/7 (today it runs from a manual script).
  • Close the loop — let the agent auto-hold a flagged receipt, draft a re-price, or raise a Recipe Change Request behind human approval (today it alerts but doesn't act).
  • Multi-tenant + hosted demo — onboarding ETL so a new chain's menu/costing/sales feed the agents; per-tenant notification config.
  • Trust layer — cost metering, an answer-quality eval set, and an audit link from each AI suggestion to the change it drove.

Honesty note: the AI is real but narrow — ~110 of 113 routes are classic CRUD, and the agent is advisory (it flags, a human acts). Payroll is intentionally parked.

The role it replaces

A junior cost-controller.

The manual report-pulling and reconciliation a food-cost / inventory-audit seat does — catching the wrong-unit receipt at the door, the price creep, the margin leak — running continuously instead of monthly.
